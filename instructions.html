    Rules

    Eligibility for EncipherX CTF


  • The EncipherX CTF is open to all undergraduate and postgraduate professionals, making it an inclusive International event.

    • Rules for the contest


  • Teams participating in the CTF must refrain from conducting any attacks on the CTF infrastructure. This includes, but is not limited to, DoS, DDoS, bruteforcing, automated scans, or generating excessive traffic through any means on challenges or other contest infrastructure. Such activities are strictly prohibited and not intended in any question.

  • Teams competing in offline mode are expected to maintain discipline within the college campus.

  • Collaboration between different teams and sharing of flags is not allowed.

  • Participants/Teams are strictly prohibited from publishing solutions during the event on platforms such as blogs, GitHub, YouTube, etc.

  • Any participants/Teams found violating the aforementioned rules will be disqualified from the contest, and strict action will be taken.

  • Team EncipherX reserves the right to introduce new rules or modify existing rules during or after the contest. These rules will apply to all participants in the contest.

  • The decisions made by Team Encipher X will be final.

  • By participating in the contest, you acknowledge that you have read and fully agree to adhere to the above rules.

  • While participants are allowed to use tools, references, and snippets publicly available on the Internet, seeking help from people, forums, etc., for solving a challenge is strictly prohibited.
